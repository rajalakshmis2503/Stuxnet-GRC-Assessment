# Stuxnet-GRC-Assessment
# Could Stuxnet Happen Today?

Often referred to as the world's first cyber weapon, Stuxnet fundamentally changed how governments, security researchers, and organizations viewed cyber conflict. The incident demonstrated that malicious code could move beyond data theft and disruption to produce real-world physical consequences within critical infrastructure.

This project examines Stuxnet from a Governance, Risk and Compliance (GRC) perspective, focusing on the governance failures, operational weaknesses, and security control gaps that enabled the attack to succeed.

Using the Stuxnet attack chain as a case study, I developed a risk assessment, mapped findings against NIST CSF 2.0 and ISO/IEC 27001, and evaluated whether similar risks remain relevant within modern operational technology environments.

### Key Finding

The success of Stuxnet cannot be attributed to advanced malware alone. The attack was enabled by a combination of weak governance, inadequate monitoring, poor access management, ineffective change control, and limited visibility into critical systems.

**More than a decade later, Stuxnet remains relevant not because of the malware itself, but because the governance and control failures it exposed continue to challenge organizations today..**
